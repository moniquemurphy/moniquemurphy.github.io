Mixed Nuts
In this society where we're all like nuts crammed into a bag,
We meet other people and snuggle close to them.
Blending in with all the others, we pretended to be
Fruits instead of peanuts, smiling.

On a template for happiness, literally the very picture of "what lies beneath the surface,"
Even when we hold hands, sitting around a table, things can't be the way they are.

It's all full of secrets, a home that's been patched together, you know?
Our true feelings stuck between our teeth, even if you bite down they don't come out,
Full of worry, letting life flow on its own, and I know,
The days are coated with the trivial, but I'm here, and you're here,
Just that truth alone lies heavy in my stomach.

Like a peanut unmasked,
Repelled from society in a single moment, it's especially at those times
That I ask that I be allowed to be by your side, even thought it's not perfect,
Let us be roasted together, shaken, even if we're stepped on, I'll become like a hard shell that doesn't break.

You're either born on a tree or in the ground, that's the only distinction,
I hope this world, so dry, hard, and unforgiving, is soaked with rain all over.

Sometimes, beyond the cold, in the liveliness beyond the window, you know?
I can't see a single star, I wish for days full of lightning,
I make so many mistakes, give me a show made of ad libs, but I know,
After all, it's just a piece of the everyday, I'll let it pass through my stomach and go to sleep.

It's all full of secrets, a home that's been patched together, you know?
I'll consume everything, flawless custom or ready-made,
In life, there's no such thing as ordinary, there's no such thing as correct, and I know,
The days are coated with the trivial, but I'm here, and you're here,
Just that truth alone lies heavy in my stomach.
I'll bite down on this handful of miracles.
