Song Bits, Echo
You wonder, who dripped a touch of indigo
On the red spider lily on her sleeve?
The night melts away brilliant colors, but a crimson moon hangs in the sky.

Running so fast it's like I split the air itself,
I got stronger every time I stumbled,
I embrace the light, the pain, the anger, all of it,
I have to choose, I can choose.

Voices, ring out, far away in the night,
Ring out to the distant scenery, soaked with tears,
What song will you play? Who do you want to hear?
It doesn't matter if it's indistinct,
No matter how dark you feel, no matter how long the conflict goes on,
The echo dissolves with the song.

I dance alone for a thousand nights,
If you tie on the unchanging sash,
Burn, crimson incense, so that you don't lose to the brilliant colors.

No matter what painful times lay ahead,
Before you speak, prepare yourself,
All you have to do is follow the light that your dreams broadcast,
Didn't you come this far so that you could run away?

You have to choose, you are allowed to choose.

Starting with your voice, like a burning flower,
If you light up the darkness,
The too-vague correct answer also becomes sheet music,
Count the nights, like you're going to sketch the morning,
Ring out a brilliant sound,
No matter how deep your regrets, no matter how high the limits,
Erase them, echo.
